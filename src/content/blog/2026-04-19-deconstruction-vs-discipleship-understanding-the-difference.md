> "Dear friends, although I was very eager to write to you about the salvation we share, I felt compelled to write and urge you to contend for the faith that was once for all entrusted to God's holy people."  
> — **Jude 1:3**

Deconstruction has become a buzzword, and like most buzzwords, it covers a lot of very different things under one umbrella. For some people, it means questioning the harmful extras their church added on top of the gospel - bad politics, controlling leaders, legalism. That kind of questioning is often healthy and biblical. For others, it has become a slow, social-media-fueled exit ramp from Christianity itself. Those are not the same journey.

Healthy discipleship has always involved examining what you were taught. The Bereans were praised for fact-checking even the apostle Paul against Scripture. Wrestling with hard passages, sorting cultural baggage from biblical truth, asking why you believe what you believe - this is how faith matures. The goal is not to reject the faith, but to refine it, so what remains is more solid, not less.

Deconstruction becomes dangerous when it stops being honest investigation and starts being a performance. When the point becomes tearing everything down without rebuilding, when cynicism replaces curiosity, when your new community is built more on what you have rejected than on what you believe, you are not really pursuing truth anymore. You are pursuing a new tribe, and that tribe often leaves people more lost than they were.

If you are in a season of questioning, do not run it alone on TikTok. Get into serious books, serious Scripture, and serious community with believers who have wrestled and stayed. Ask hard questions out loud. Be willing to let God be bigger than your old categories and also bigger than your new objections. Real faith can survive honest scrutiny. In fact, that is usually how it becomes unshakable.

## Taking It Further

Knowing truth is only half the battle. Living it is where the rubber meets the road. So what does this look like in your life this week? It might mean having a hard conversation you've been avoiding. It might mean writing down a promise and reading it every morning before your feet hit the floor. It might mean simply pausing at the end of the day to ask one honest question: Did I walk this out today?

Scripture is a living word — and it changes lives when we put it into practice. Don't let today's reading stay on the page. Let it travel with you into every conversation, every decision, every quiet moment. That's when the gospel stops being information and starts being transformation. And that's the kind of faith worth rocking on your sleeve — literally.

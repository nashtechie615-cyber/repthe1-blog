> "But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, forbearance, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness and self-control. Against such things there is no law."  
> — **Galatians 5:22-23**

Patience in 2026 is a countercultural act. We live in a world where food arrives in minutes, answers arrive in seconds, and any delay feels like a personal insult. Our thumbs have been trained to refresh. Our hearts have been trained to demand. And yet Scripture calls patience a fruit of the Spirit, which means it grows slowly on purpose.

Real patience is not just waiting without complaining. It is trusting that God is working even when the timeline is not yours. It is refusing to force a door that is not opening. It is holding your tongue with the coworker who drives you crazy, and staying at the table with the family member who has disappointed you again, because love is long before it is anything else.

Patience gets formed in traffic, in waiting rooms, in slow answers to prayer. It gets formed when the promotion does not come, when the spouse is still not on the same page, when the diagnosis is still unclear. God uses delay the way a farmer uses winter. Nothing looks alive, but everything is being prepared.

Today, pick one place where you have been impatient and reframe it. Instead of asking God to hurry, ask Him what He is growing in you through the wait. Then take a breath, unclench your jaw, and stay. Patience is not passive. It is active trust that the One who started the story knows how to finish it.

## Taking It Further

Knowing truth is only half the battle. Living it is where the rubber meets the road. So what does this look like in your life this week? It might mean having a hard conversation you've been avoiding. It might mean writing down a promise and reading it every morning before your feet hit the floor. It might mean simply pausing at the end of the day to ask one honest question: Did I walk this out today?

Scripture is a living word — and it changes lives when we put it into practice. Don't let today's reading stay on the page. Let it travel with you into every conversation, every decision, every quiet moment. That's when the gospel stops being information and starts being transformation. And that's the kind of faith worth rocking on your sleeve — literally.

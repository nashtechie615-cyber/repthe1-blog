> "But those who hope in the Lord will renew their strength. They will soar on wings like eagles; they will run and not grow weary, they will walk and not be faint."  
> — **Isaiah 40:31**

Waiting is one of the hardest things God asks us to do. We live in a world of instant everything - instant food, instant answers, instant gratification. But God operates on a different timeline.

The waiting room of God is not wasted time. It is training ground. It is where patience is forged, faith is deepened, and character is built.

Abraham waited 25 years for the promised son. Joseph spent years in prison before the palace. David was anointed king long before he wore the crown.

If you are in a season of waiting, take heart. God is not slow. He is strategic. And His timing is always, always worth the wait.

## Taking It Further

Knowing truth is only half the battle. Living it is where the rubber meets the road. So what does this look like in your life this week? It might mean having a hard conversation you've been avoiding. It might mean writing down a promise and reading it every morning before your feet hit the floor. It might mean simply pausing at the end of the day to ask one honest question: Did I walk this out today?

Scripture is a living word — and it changes lives when we put it into practice. Don't let today's reading stay on the page. Let it travel with you into every conversation, every decision, every quiet moment. That's when the gospel stops being information and starts being transformation. And that's the kind of faith worth rocking on your sleeve — literally.

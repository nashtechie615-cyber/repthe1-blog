> "Bear with each other and forgive one another if any of you has a grievance against someone. Forgive as the Lord forgave you."  
> — **Colossians 3:13**

Forgiveness is one of the most counterintuitive commands in Scripture. Someone hurts you deeply, and God says... let it go? It feels unfair. It feels impossible.

But here is what unforgiveness really does: it chains you to the person who hurt you. It replays the offense on loop. It poisons your peace and steals your joy.

Forgiveness is not saying what they did was okay. It is saying I am not going to carry this anymore. It is releasing the debt to God and trusting Him to handle justice.

Jesus forgave from the cross. While bleeding. While suffering. If He can forgive in that moment, He can give you the strength to forgive in yours.

## Taking It Further

Knowing truth is only half the battle. Living it is where the rubber meets the road. So what does this look like in your life this week? It might mean having a hard conversation you've been avoiding. It might mean writing down a promise and reading it every morning before your feet hit the floor. It might mean simply pausing at the end of the day to ask one honest question: Did I walk this out today?

Scripture is a living word — and it changes lives when we put it into practice. Don't let today's reading stay on the page. Let it travel with you into every conversation, every decision, every quiet moment. That's when the gospel stops being information and starts being transformation. And that's the kind of faith worth rocking on your sleeve — literally.

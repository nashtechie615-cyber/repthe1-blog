> "In everything I did, I showed you that by this kind of hard work we must help the weak, remembering the words the Lord Jesus himself said: It is more blessed to give than to receive."  
> — **Acts 20:35**

The world says hoard. Protect. Accumulate. Get yours. But Jesus flipped the script entirely: the most blessed life is the most generous life.

Generosity is not just about money. It is about time. Attention. Kindness. Forgiveness. It is holding everything with open hands because you trust that God is your provider.

When you give, something shifts inside you. The grip of materialism loosens. The fear of not enough fades. You start to see people not as competition but as neighbors.

You do not have to be wealthy to be generous. Start where you are. Give what you have. Watch God multiply it in ways you never expected.

## Taking It Further

Knowing truth is only half the battle. Living it is where the rubber meets the road. So what does this look like in your life this week? It might mean having a hard conversation you've been avoiding. It might mean writing down a promise and reading it every morning before your feet hit the floor. It might mean simply pausing at the end of the day to ask one honest question: Did I walk this out today?

Scripture is a living word — and it changes lives when we put it into practice. Don't let today's reading stay on the page. Let it travel with you into every conversation, every decision, every quiet moment. That's when the gospel stops being information and starts being transformation. And that's the kind of faith worth rocking on your sleeve — literally.

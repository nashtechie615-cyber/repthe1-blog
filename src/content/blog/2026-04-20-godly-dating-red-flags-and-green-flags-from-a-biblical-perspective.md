> "Do not be yoked together with unbelievers. For what do righteousness and wickedness have in common? Or what fellowship can light have with darkness?"  
> — **2 Corinthians 6:14**

Dating as a Christian in 2026 is a strange mix of apps, coffee dates, and confusion. It is easy to settle because you are lonely, or to obsess because the clock feels loud. Before you figure out whether this person is the one, you have to figure out whether you are asking the right questions in the first place. Compatibility matters, but character matters more.

Green flags are not about chemistry alone. They are about a person whose walk with Christ is real even when you are not watching. They serve their family well. They handle conflict without manipulation. They take responsibility for their own sin without dragging you into theirs. They are moving somewhere with their life, and they make you want to follow Jesus more, not less.

Red flags are easy to rationalize when you like someone. A harsh temper behind closed doors. Secret habits they will not discuss. A faith that only shows up for your benefit. An unwillingness to submit to anyone or any wisdom outside themselves. Pay attention. Infatuation lies. Your future self is begging you to notice what your present self keeps explaining away.

Before the next date, write down three non-negotiables rooted in Scripture - not preferences, like height or hobbies, but real character markers. Shared faith. Integrity. A willingness to grow. Hold dates to those. You are not being picky. You are being stewarded. The person you marry will shape the rest of your walk with Jesus. That is worth being slow about.

## Taking It Further

Knowing truth is only half the battle. Living it is where the rubber meets the road. So what does this look like in your life this week? It might mean having a hard conversation you've been avoiding. It might mean writing down a promise and reading it every morning before your feet hit the floor. It might mean simply pausing at the end of the day to ask one honest question: Did I walk this out today?

Scripture is a living word — and it changes lives when we put it into practice. Don't let today's reading stay on the page. Let it travel with you into every conversation, every decision, every quiet moment. That's when the gospel stops being information and starts being transformation. And that's the kind of faith worth rocking on your sleeve — literally.

> "For he chose us in him before the creation of the world to be holy and blameless in his sight. In love he predestined us for adoption to sonship through Jesus Christ."  
> — **Ephesians 1:4-5**

The world has never stopped telling you who you are. You are your follower count. You are your job title. You are your biggest mistake or your best photo. You are whatever the loudest voice in your head has been repeating since you were twelve. These labels stick because they are familiar, not because they are true.

Scripture pulls the rug out from under every one of them. In Christ, you are chosen, adopted, forgiven, sealed, and seated. You are called a saint before you ever felt saintly. You are named a son or daughter of the living God, and that is not a motivational phrase. It is a legal reality purchased at the cross.

When you forget who you are, you start acting like who the world says you are. You chase approval. You hide from failure. You measure your worth against someone else's highlight reel. But when your identity is anchored in Christ, the approval game loses its grip, because the only verdict that matters has already been delivered: beloved.

Write this down somewhere you will see it. I am chosen. I am forgiven. I am His. Say it out loud in the mirror if you have to. Every time the old label tries to reattach, speak the truth that is older and stronger. You are not who the world says you are. You are who God says you are.

## Taking It Further

Knowing truth is only half the battle. Living it is where the rubber meets the road. So what does this look like in your life this week? It might mean having a hard conversation you've been avoiding. It might mean writing down a promise and reading it every morning before your feet hit the floor. It might mean simply pausing at the end of the day to ask one honest question: Did I walk this out today?

Scripture is a living word — and it changes lives when we put it into practice. Don't let today's reading stay on the page. Let it travel with you into every conversation, every decision, every quiet moment. That's when the gospel stops being information and starts being transformation. And that's the kind of faith worth rocking on your sleeve — literally.

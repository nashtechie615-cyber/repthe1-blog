> "Be on your guard; stand firm in the faith; be courageous; be strong."  
> — **1 Corinthians 16:13**

Living out your faith in today's culture is not for the faint of heart. You will be questioned. You will be mocked. You will be told your beliefs are outdated, intolerant, or irrelevant.

But here is the thing - the early church faced the same pressure. And they turned the world upside down. Not with political power or cultural influence, but with unwavering love and unshakable conviction.

Standing firm does not mean being combative. It means being rooted. Knowing what you believe and why you believe it. Responding to hostility with grace and truth in equal measure.

You were not called to blend in. You were called to stand out. And standing out for Jesus has always been the most countercultural thing you can do.

## Taking It Further

Knowing truth is only half the battle. Living it is where the rubber meets the road. So what does this look like in your life this week? It might mean having a hard conversation you've been avoiding. It might mean writing down a promise and reading it every morning before your feet hit the floor. It might mean simply pausing at the end of the day to ask one honest question: Did I walk this out today?

Scripture is a living word — and it changes lives when we put it into practice. Don't let today's reading stay on the page. Let it travel with you into every conversation, every decision, every quiet moment. That's when the gospel stops being information and starts being transformation. And that's the kind of faith worth rocking on your sleeve — literally.

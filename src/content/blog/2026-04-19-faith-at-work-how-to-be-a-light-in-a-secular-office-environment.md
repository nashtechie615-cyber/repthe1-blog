> "In the same way, let your light shine before others, that they may see your good deeds and glorify your Father in heaven."  
> — **Matthew 5:16**

Most of your waking hours are spent at work, surrounded by people who may not share your faith and may not want to. You are not there by accident. God did not misplace you. The cubicle, the job site, the Zoom call - these are the mission field for most believers, and the way you show up there preaches louder than anything you could say.

Being a light does not mean cornering your coworker at the coffee machine with a tract. It means being the person who keeps their word, owns their mistakes, does not gossip, and treats the janitor with the same respect as the CEO. It means being the calm one when the deadline hits and the project goes sideways. Integrity gets noticed long before any verse you quote.

When conversations open naturally, be ready to speak simply about your hope. Not a seminary lecture. Not a debate. Just honest words about why you are not panicking, or why you forgave that client, or why Sunday morning is non-negotiable. People are watching for something that is real, and a gentle, lived-out faith is almost always more compelling than a polished argument.

This week, pray for three coworkers by name. Do not tell them. Just start asking God to move in their lives and to use you however He wants. Then go to work and do your job with excellence and kindness. That steady witness, repeated over months and years, is how most people come to faith - not from a sermon, but from a neighbor.

## Taking It Further

Knowing truth is only half the battle. Living it is where the rubber meets the road. So what does this look like in your life this week? It might mean having a hard conversation you've been avoiding. It might mean writing down a promise and reading it every morning before your feet hit the floor. It might mean simply pausing at the end of the day to ask one honest question: Did I walk this out today?

Scripture is a living word — and it changes lives when we put it into practice. Don't let today's reading stay on the page. Let it travel with you into every conversation, every decision, every quiet moment. That's when the gospel stops being information and starts being transformation. And that's the kind of faith worth rocking on your sleeve — literally.

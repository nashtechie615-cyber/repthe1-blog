> "Start children off on the way they should go, and even when they are old they will not turn from it."  
> — **Proverbs 22:6**

Parenting is the hardest and most important job you will ever have. And in a world competing for your child's attention 24/7, raising them in faith feels more urgent than ever.

It does not require perfection. Kids do not need perfect parents - they need present ones. They need to see you pray when times are hard. They need to hear you worship when things are good.

Family devotions do not have to look like a seminary class. Read a verse at dinner. Pray in the car. Talk about God as naturally as you talk about everything else.

The seeds you plant today may not bloom tomorrow. But they will bloom. Trust the gardener. He is faithful with what you sow.

## Taking It Further

Knowing truth is only half the battle. Living it is where the rubber meets the road. So what does this look like in your life this week? It might mean having a hard conversation you've been avoiding. It might mean writing down a promise and reading it every morning before your feet hit the floor. It might mean simply pausing at the end of the day to ask one honest question: Did I walk this out today?

Scripture is a living word — and it changes lives when we put it into practice. Don't let today's reading stay on the page. Let it travel with you into every conversation, every decision, every quiet moment. That's when the gospel stops being information and starts being transformation. And that's the kind of faith worth rocking on your sleeve — literally.

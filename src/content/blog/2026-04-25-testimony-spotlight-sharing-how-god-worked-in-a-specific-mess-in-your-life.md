> "They triumphed over him by the blood of the Lamb and by the word of their testimony; they did not love their lives so much as to shrink from death."  
> — **Revelation 12:11**

Your testimony is not just the moment you first believed. It is the long list of specific things God has carried you through since then. The job loss that forced you to pray differently. The marriage that almost ended and did not. The season of depression you are still clawing out of. Every one of those chapters is evidence of His faithfulness, and you do not have to wait until the story is tidy to talk about it.

The enemy loves silence around our messes, because silence is where shame grows. When you speak honestly about how God met you in something specific, two things happen. You build up your own faith, because telling the story reminds you it was real. And you give someone else permission to believe that their own mess is not outside the reach of grace.

Your testimony does not have to be polished or dramatic. It does not have to involve a rock-bottom movie scene. Sometimes the most powerful testimonies are simple. I was anxious all the time, and over two years of counseling and prayer, God taught me how to rest. I was gripping my money too tightly, and He loosened my grip through generosity. Small, specific, true. That is gold.

This week, write down one specific way God has worked in your life in the last few years. Not a sermon. Just the story, in your own words, in one page or less. Then pray about who might need to hear it. Share it with one person - a friend, a family member, a stranger at the right moment. You are a walking piece of evidence. Do not hide what He has done.

## Taking It Further

Knowing truth is only half the battle. Living it is where the rubber meets the road. So what does this look like in your life this week? It might mean having a hard conversation you've been avoiding. It might mean writing down a promise and reading it every morning before your feet hit the floor. It might mean simply pausing at the end of the day to ask one honest question: Did I walk this out today?

Scripture is a living word — and it changes lives when we put it into practice. Don't let today's reading stay on the page. Let it travel with you into every conversation, every decision, every quiet moment. That's when the gospel stops being information and starts being transformation. And that's the kind of faith worth rocking on your sleeve — literally.

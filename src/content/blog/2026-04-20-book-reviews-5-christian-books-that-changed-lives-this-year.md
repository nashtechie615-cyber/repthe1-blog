> "Listen to advice and accept discipline, and at the end you will be counted among the wise."  
> — **Proverbs 19:20**

A good Christian book is not a substitute for the Bible, but it can be one of the sharpest tools God uses to grow you. Reading slowly, underlining, and thinking alongside older and wiser believers stretches your mind in ways fast-scrolling content never will. Many of the people you admire in the faith got that way partly because they stayed in conversation with serious books for years.

There are a few categories worth keeping on rotation. A classic, like something from C.S. Lewis or Augustine, to anchor you in the long tradition. A spiritual formation book to help you pray, rest, and be with God. A theology book that is thick enough to make you think. A biography of a believer whose life challenges you. And a book on a specific struggle you are facing right now.

Reading is a discipline, not a hobby. It takes practice. Start smaller than you think - twenty minutes a day, one chapter at a time. Keep a pen in your hand. Write what God is teaching you in the margin or a notebook. After six months, you will look back and realize the slow drip of truth has changed how you think about work, family, suffering, and God Himself.

This week, pick one Christian book from a trusted source and start it. Not just listen to it at double speed while you do dishes - actually sit with it for a few minutes a day. Take notes. Share what you are learning with a friend or small group. The books that changed lives this year did not do it by magic. They did it because ordinary readers slowed down long enough to let them.

## Taking It Further

Knowing truth is only half the battle. Living it is where the rubber meets the road. So what does this look like in your life this week? It might mean having a hard conversation you've been avoiding. It might mean writing down a promise and reading it every morning before your feet hit the floor. It might mean simply pausing at the end of the day to ask one honest question: Did I walk this out today?

Scripture is a living word — and it changes lives when we put it into practice. Don't let today's reading stay on the page. Let it travel with you into every conversation, every decision, every quiet moment. That's when the gospel stops being information and starts being transformation. And that's the kind of faith worth rocking on your sleeve — literally.

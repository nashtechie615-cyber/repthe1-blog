> "In the same way, the Spirit helps us in our weakness. We do not know what we ought to pray for, but the Spirit himself intercedes for us through wordless groans."  
> — **Romans 8:26**

Have you ever sat down to pray and had absolutely nothing to say? The words feel stuck. The ceiling feels low. The silence feels heavy.

Here is the good news: prayer is not a performance. It is a relationship. And in every relationship, sometimes you just sit together in silence. God is not grading your prayers for eloquence.

The Holy Spirit intercedes for you even when your words fail. Your sighs are prayers. Your tears are prayers. Your honest I do not know what to say, God is a prayer.

Do not let the enemy convince you that your prayers are not good enough. Just show up. God meets you where you are, not where you think you should be.

## Taking It Further

Knowing truth is only half the battle. Living it is where the rubber meets the road. So what does this look like in your life this week? It might mean having a hard conversation you've been avoiding. It might mean writing down a promise and reading it every morning before your feet hit the floor. It might mean simply pausing at the end of the day to ask one honest question: Did I walk this out today?

Scripture is a living word — and it changes lives when we put it into practice. Don't let today's reading stay on the page. Let it travel with you into every conversation, every decision, every quiet moment. That's when the gospel stops being information and starts being transformation. And that's the kind of faith worth rocking on your sleeve — literally.

> "For I know the plans I have for you, declares the Lord, plans to prosper you and not to harm you, plans to give you hope and a future."  
> — **Jeremiah 29:11**

You are not an accident. You are not a random collection of cells floating through space. You were created on purpose, with purpose, for a purpose.

The enemy wants you to believe your life does not matter. That you are too broken, too old, too young, too far gone. But God says otherwise.

Your purpose is not always a grand, public calling. Sometimes it is being a faithful parent. A loyal friend. A light in a dark office. Purpose is found in faithfulness to the small things.

Stop waiting for some dramatic moment to live on purpose. Start today. Love the person in front of you. Serve where you are planted. That is kingdom work.

## Taking It Further

Knowing truth is only half the battle. Living it is where the rubber meets the road. So what does this look like in your life this week? It might mean having a hard conversation you've been avoiding. It might mean writing down a promise and reading it every morning before your feet hit the floor. It might mean simply pausing at the end of the day to ask one honest question: Did I walk this out today?

Scripture is a living word — and it changes lives when we put it into practice. Don't let today's reading stay on the page. Let it travel with you into every conversation, every decision, every quiet moment. That's when the gospel stops being information and starts being transformation. And that's the kind of faith worth rocking on your sleeve — literally.

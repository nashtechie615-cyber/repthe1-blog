> "The second is this: Love your neighbor as yourself. There is no commandment greater than these."  
> — **Mark 12:31**

Love your neighbor sounds simple until your neighbor is difficult. Until they have different politics. Until they play music too loud at midnight. Until they post things on social media that make your blood boil.

But Jesus did not say love your neighbor when it is convenient. He said love your neighbor, period. And He modeled it by loving people who actively tried to destroy Him.

In 2026, loving your neighbor might look like putting down your phone and having a real conversation. Bringing groceries to the single mom next door. Praying for the coworker who irritates you.

Love is not a feeling - it is a decision. And every decision to love, no matter how small, echoes into eternity.

## Taking It Further

Knowing truth is only half the battle. Living it is where the rubber meets the road. So what does this look like in your life this week? It might mean having a hard conversation you've been avoiding. It might mean writing down a promise and reading it every morning before your feet hit the floor. It might mean simply pausing at the end of the day to ask one honest question: Did I walk this out today?

Scripture is a living word — and it changes lives when we put it into practice. Don't let today's reading stay on the page. Let it travel with you into every conversation, every decision, every quiet moment. That's when the gospel stops being information and starts being transformation. And that's the kind of faith worth rocking on your sleeve — literally.
